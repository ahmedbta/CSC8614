 My reflections of the Outfoxed podcast with Adam Goldfarb ( https://hunter.io/blog/adam-goldfarb-on-why-most-cold-email-is-a-nuisance/?utm_campaign=2025-12+Outfoxed%3A+Episode+five&utm_content=Adam+episode+reflections&utm_medium=email_action&utm_source=customer.io )How do you get a 20% positive reply rate from cold email in 2026?

The unobvious answer is: you stop acting like a “cold emailer.”

In Outfoxed episode 5, we sat down with Adam Goldfarb, and he shared the closest thing I’ve heard to a 2026 definition of doing cold email well: be an anti–cold emailer.

I recently wrote my full breakdown, and the point isn’t theory.

If a 20% reply rate sounds like your dream cold email performance, this blog turns Adam's process into practical experiments you can run in January to move your reply rates.

Read my biggest takeaways ( https://hunter.io/blog/adam-goldfarb-on-why-most-cold-email-is-a-nuisance/?utm_campaign=2025-12+Outfoxed%3A+Episode+five&utm_content=Adam+episode+reflections&utm_medium=email_action&utm_source=customer.io ) 

Here’s what you’ll learn + test:

*
The Nuisance Test (aka “is this spam?”): Adam’s definition is brutal: if your email is a nuisance, it’s spam. Read the blog for a simple scoring checklist to run before you hit send.
* The 80/20 rule: research beats writing: Spend 80% of your time finding the right people and 20% writing the email. I share a “micro-list sprint” you can run with 50 leads.
*
Lead with credibility: Your opening sentence should prove you get their world. The blog includes a “credibility bank” framework you can build once and reuse.
* Shorter wins (3–4 sentences): Not “perfect copy.” Not a long pitch. Just enough to earn permission to continue. I share a clean structure you can A/B test.
