Now is the perfect time to put Adam's advice to the test.

That means sending cold emails that your leads truly want, and in our January playbook, we're sharing 10 plays to do that:

* Micro-list constraint: Email 10–25 people in one niche (role + industry + trigger) and say you’re only reaching out to that small set.
* One pain, three receipts: Use 3 “receipts” (G2 reviews, job posts, community threads) that prove a shared pain—ask if it’s true for them.
*
Timing trigger outreach: Only email when there’s a real trigger (hiring, expansion, funding, product shift, competitor move).
* Permission-first Loom: Ask if you can send a 90-second Loom—don’t include it in email #1.
* Two-week pilot with boundaries: Offer a tight-scope, measurable 2-week trial (clear inputs/outputs, no vague “free consulting”).
*
Roundtable research invite: Invite to a small, role-specific roundtable with “no pitch—just shared tactics.”
* Niche benchmark/teardown asset: Share a genuinely useful mini-asset (benchmarks, teardown, checklist) and ask permission to send.
* Single-question diagnostic: Ask one expert-level question that reveals fit and starts a real conversation.
*
Multichannel courtesy: Connect on LinkedIn first, then email referencing the request to create real context.
* Three-email maximum: Tell them you’ll stop after 3 emails—and actually stop.
